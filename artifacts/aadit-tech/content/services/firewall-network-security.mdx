## How a Firewall Protects Your Network

A firewall acts as a gatekeeper, inspecting incoming and outgoing traffic against defined rules and blocking anything malicious. Modern firewalls use several techniques:

- **Packet Filtering** — inspect individual packets by source, destination, port, and protocol.
- **Stateful Inspection** — track the state of connections to allow only legitimate traffic.
- **Proxy Firewalls** — mediate traffic and hide your internal network from external users.
- **Next-Generation Firewalls (NGFWs)** — add intrusion prevention, application control, and deep packet inspection.
