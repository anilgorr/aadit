faqs:
  - question: What is backup and disaster recovery?
    answer: Backup and disaster recovery (BDR) is a comprehensive strategy to protect your business from data loss and system downtime. It involves creating copies of your critical data and storing them securely, and developing a plan for recovering your systems and data after a disaster. Backup focuses on data duplication and restoration, while disaster recovery covers broader business continuity.
  - question: How much does backup and disaster recovery cost?
    answer: The cost varies depending on the size and complexity of your business, the amount of data you need to protect, and the type of solution you choose. Factors include storage space, bandwidth usage, software licensing, and the level of managed services required. Contact us for a personalised quote.
  - question: What are the different types of backup and disaster recovery?
    answer: The main types are onsite backup, offsite backup, cloud backup, hybrid backup, Disaster Recovery as a Service (DRaaS), and managed backup and disaster recovery. The right mix depends on your recovery goals and budget.
  - question: How do I create a backup and disaster recovery plan?
    answer: Creating a BDR plan involves a risk assessment, a business impact analysis, setting RTOs and RPOs, selecting the right backup and recovery solutions, documenting the plan, testing and validating it, and keeping it up to date as your business and technology change.
  - question: What are Recovery Time Objectives (RTOs) and Recovery Point Objectives (RPOs)?
    answer: RTO is the maximum acceptable downtime for a system or application after a disaster — how long your business can operate without that resource. RPO is the maximum acceptable data loss measured in time — how far back you can tolerate losing data. Setting appropriate RTOs and RPOs is crucial for an effective BDR plan.
  - question: Why is offsite data backup storage important?
    answer: Offsite storage protects your data from local disasters such as fires, floods, and theft. If your onsite backups are destroyed along with your primary data, you would not be able to recover. Storing backups in a geographically diverse location keeps your data safe even during a widespread disaster.
  - question: What is Disaster Recovery as a Service (DRaaS)?
    answer: DRaaS is a cloud-based solution that replicates your critical systems and data to a remote location so you can quickly recover them after a disaster. The provider handles data replication, failover, and failback, offering a cost-effective and reliable path to business continuity.

## The Harsh Realities of Data Loss and Downtime

A flooded server room, a corrupted database, or a ransomware attack that encrypts your files can lead to lost revenue, damaged reputation, and even business closure. A significant share of businesses that experience major data loss never fully recover, and downtime carries a heavy cost. Backup and disaster recovery is no longer optional — it is a fundamental requirement for survival in today's digital landscape.
