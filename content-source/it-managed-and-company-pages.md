## FAQs

**What is backup and disaster recovery?**
Backup and disaster recovery (BDR) is a comprehensive strategy to protect your business from data loss and system downtime. It involves creating copies of your critical data and storing them in a secure location, as well as developing a plan for recovering your systems and data in the event of a disaster. Backup focuses on data duplication and restoration, while disaster recovery encompasses broader strategies for business continuity.

**How much does backup and disaster recovery cost?**
The cost of BDR varies greatly depending on the size and complexity of your business, the amount of data you need to protect, and the type of solution you choose. Factors influencing the cost include storage space, bandwidth usage, software licensing, and the level of managed services required. Aadit Technologies offers flexible pricing options to fit your budget and needs. Contact us for a personalized quote.

**What are the different types of backup and disaster recovery?**
- Onsite Backup: Storing data backups on-premises.
- Offsite Backup: Storing backups in a remote location.
- Cloud Backup: Storing backups in the cloud.
- Hybrid Backup: Combining onsite and offsite backup.
- Disaster Recovery as a Service (DRaaS): Cloud-based disaster recovery services.
- Managed Backup and Disaster Recovery: Outsourcing BDR to a managed service provide

**What are the best backup and disaster recovery solutions?**
The "best" BDR solution depends on your specific needs and requirements. Factors to consider include your budget, recovery time objectives (RTOs), recovery point objectives (RPOs), the type of data you need to protect, and your compliance requirements. Aadit Technologies can help you assess your needs and recommend the best solution for your business

**How do I create a backup and disaster recovery plan?**
Creating a BDR plan involves several steps, including:
1. Risk Assessment: Identify potential threats and vulnerabilities.
2. Business Impact Analysis: Determine the impact of downtime on your business.
3. Setting RTOs and RPOs: Define your recovery goals.
4. Selecting Backup and Recovery Solutions: Choose the right technologies for your needs.
5. Documenting Your Plan: Create a detailed plan for responding to and recovering from disasters.
6. Testing and Validation: Regularly test your plan to ensure it works.
7. Maintaining and Updating: Keep your plan up-to-date with changes in your business and technology.

**What are Recovery Time Objectives (RTOs) and Recovery Point Objectives (RPOs)?**
Recovery Time Objective (RTO) is the maximum acceptable downtime for a system or application after a disaster. It defines how long your business can survive without that particular resource. Recovery Point Objective (RPO) is the maximum acceptable data loss measured in time. It defines how far back you can tolerate losing data in the event of a disaster. Setting appropriate RTOs and RPOs is crucial for designing an effective BDR plan.

**Why is offsite data backup storage important?**
Offsite data backup storage is crucial because it protects your data from local disasters such as fires, floods, and theft. If your onsite backups are destroyed along with your primary data, you will not be able to recover your business. Storing backups in a geographically diverse location ensures that your data is safe, even in the event of a widespread disaster.

**What is Disaster Recovery as a Service (DRaaS)?**
Disaster Recovery as a Service (DRaaS) is a cloud-based solution that allows you to replicate your critical systems and data to a remote location and quickly recover them in the event of a disaster. DRaaS providers handle all aspects of disaster recovery, from data replication to failover and failback, providing a cost-effective and reliable solution for business continuity.
